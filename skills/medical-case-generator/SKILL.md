# Clinical Case Scenario Generator

## Overview

The Clinical Case Scenario Generator creates realistic, comprehensive clinical case scenarios for medical education, simulation-based training, and clinical reasoning practice. Each case follows a structured format that mirrors real clinical encounters, progressing from initial presentation through history-taking, physical examination, diagnostic workup, differential diagnosis, and evidence-based management.

## Clinical Context

Clinical reasoning is the cornerstone of medical practice. Problem-based learning (PBL) and case-based teaching have been foundational to medical education since their introduction at McMaster University (Barrows & Tamblyn, 1980). Well-constructed clinical cases allow learners to practice hypothesis generation, data interpretation, and management planning in a low-risk environment.

This tool generates cases with realistic clinical details, appropriate vital signs, laboratory values, and imaging findings that support or refute each differential diagnosis. Management plans follow current practice guidelines.

## Features

- **Specialty-Specific Cases:** Internal medicine, emergency medicine, pediatrics, surgery, OB/GYN, psychiatry, neurology, and more
- **Complexity Grading:** Basic (early clinical students), intermediate (clerkship students and interns), and advanced (senior residents and fellows)
- **Complete Case Structure:** Demographics, chief complaint, HPI, PMH, medications, allergies, social/family history, ROS, vitals, physical exam, labs, imaging, differential diagnosis, management plan, and teaching points
- **Progressive Disclosure:** Case summaries allow learners to develop their differential before revealing the diagnosis
- **High-Yield Teaching Points:** Board-relevant clinical pearls and evidence-based management rationale
- **Red Flag Identification:** Critical findings that require immediate recognition and action

## Usage

Select a specialty and complexity level to generate a case. Use the summary function for interactive teaching sessions where learners develop their own differential before reviewing the answer. Teaching points connect each case to board-relevant content.
