# Interactive Anatomy Learning Assistant

## Overview

The Anatomy Learning Assistant is an interactive educational tool designed for medical students and healthcare professionals studying human anatomy. It provides structured anatomical information organized by body region and organ system, with a strong emphasis on clinically relevant correlations that bridge the gap between basic science and clinical medicine.

## Clinical Context

Anatomy is the foundation of medical practice, from physical examination to surgical planning to image interpretation. Yet the sheer volume of anatomical detail can be overwhelming. This tool prioritizes high-yield structures and their clinical significance, focusing on the type of integrated anatomical-clinical knowledge tested on board examinations (USMLE Step 1, Step 2 CK) and applied in clinical rotations.

## Features

- **Structure Lookup:** Detailed information on anatomical structures including origin, insertion, innervation, blood supply, and clinical significance
- **Clinical Correlations:** Each structure is linked to relevant pathology, surgical considerations, and clinical examination findings
- **Region-Based and System-Based Browsing:** Navigate anatomy by body region (head/neck, thorax, abdomen, etc.) or by organ system (cardiovascular, nervous, musculoskeletal, etc.)
- **Interactive Quiz:** Board-style clinical vignette questions that test applied anatomical knowledge, with detailed explanations
- **High-Yield Flagging:** Structures most commonly tested on board examinations are marked for priority review

## Organ Systems and Regions Covered

Body regions: Head and neck, thorax, abdomen, pelvis and perineum, upper limb, lower limb, back and spinal cord, neuroanatomy. Organ systems: Musculoskeletal, cardiovascular, respiratory, gastrointestinal, genitourinary, nervous, endocrine, lymphatic/immune.

## Usage

Use the lookup function for focused study of individual structures, the list function to browse by region or system, and the quiz function for self-assessment. Clinical correlation mode extracts pathology-relevant information for integration with pathology and clinical medicine coursework.
